ESG Reporting & Risk Analytics Dashboard
Project Overview

This project focuses on building an ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) analytics and reporting system using Python and Power BI. The objective is to simulate how financial institutions and NBFCs collect, validate, and analyze sustainability data to support ESG monitoring, risk management, and BRSR-style reporting.

The project processes a large ESG dataset containing 20,000+ records and generates meaningful insights through data cleaning, ESG scoring, risk classification, and interactive dashboards.

Project Objectives

Develop an ESG analytics pipeline for sustainability reporting.

Perform data validation and preprocessing on ESG metrics.

Calculate Environmental, Social, and Governance scores.

Classify companies into ESG risk categories.

Build interactive Power BI dashboards for ESG monitoring and reporting.

The dataset contains 20,000 records of ESG indicators representing multiple companies and years.

Project Workflow
1 Data Collection

The project uses a synthetic ESG dataset simulating sustainability metrics reported by financial institutions.

2 Data Cleaning & Validation

Python was used to:

Handle missing values

Remove duplicate records

Validate ESG metric ranges

3 ESG Score Calculation

Three scores were calculated:

Environmental Score
Based on energy consumption, carbon emissions, and water usage.

Social Score
Based on workforce diversity and CSR spending.

Governance Score
Based on board independence and compliance indicators.

A Total ESG Score was calculated using weighted averages.

4 ESG Risk Classification

Companies were categorized into:

Low ESG Risk

Medium ESG Risk

High ESG Risk

This helps simulate risk monitoring for financial institutions.

Key Insights

ESG scores improved over time due to reduced emissions and improved governance.

Companies with higher board independence generally show stronger governance scores.

Increased CSR spending is positively associated with stronger social performance.

ESG risk classification helps identify companies with higher sustainability risk exposure.
